## Rotator cuff
The rotator cuff consists of four muscles: supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, and subscapularis.
- Function: stabilize the glenohumeral joint and enable rotation of the humerus.
- Supraspinatus: abduction initiation.
- Infraspinatus & teres minor: external rotation.
- Subscapularis: internal rotation.

## Cranial nerves (short)
I - Olfactory: smell.
II - Optic: vision.
III - Oculomotor: most eye movements, pupil constriction.
IV - Trochlear: superior oblique muscle (eye movement).
V - Trigeminal: face sensation, mastication.
VI - Abducens: lateral rectus muscle (abducts eye).
VII - Facial: facial expression, taste anterior 2/3 tongue.
VIII - Vestibulocochlear: hearing and balance.
IX - Glossopharyngeal: taste posterior 1/3 tongue, swallowing.
X - Vagus: parasympathetic to thorax/abdomen.
XI - Accessory: SCM & trapezius.
XII - Hypoglossal: tongue movement.
